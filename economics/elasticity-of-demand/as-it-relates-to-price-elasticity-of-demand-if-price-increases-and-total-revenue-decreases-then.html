ELASTICITY OF DEMAND

[SOURCES]
As it relates to Price Elasticity of Demand; if price increases and total revenue decreases then

(A) ** >1, relatively elastic

(B) <1, relatively inelastic

(C) = 1, unit elastic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If an increase in price causes a decrease in total revenue, then demand can be said to be elastic, since the increase in price has a large impact on quantity demanded. Different commodities may have different elasticities depending on whether people need them (necessities) or want them (accessories).

Concept note-2: -when the elasticity is greater than one, indicating that a 1 percent increase in price will result in a more than 1 percent increase in quantity; this indicates a high responsiveness to price.

Concept note-3: -Hence, when the price is raised, the total revenue falls, and vice versa. When the price elasticity of demand is perfectly elastic (Ed is − ∞), any increase in the price, no matter how small, will cause the quantity demanded for the good to drop to zero. Hence, when the price is raised, the total revenue falls to zero.

Concept note-4: -When demand is elastic (price elasticity ), price and total revenue have a negative relationship, meaning that price rises lead to lower total revenue.
