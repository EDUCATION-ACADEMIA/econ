Define discerning

(A) ** discriminating; showing intellectual perception; showing good judgment; making thoughtful and astute choices; selected for value or relevance

(B) satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity equal to the requirement or occasion

(C) successful in producing the intended, desired or expected result; meeting the assigned purpose

(D) concerned with or comprehending only what is on the surface or obvious; shallow; not profound, thorough, deep or complete; existing or occurring at or on the surface; cursory; lacking depth of character or understanding; apparent and sometimes trivial

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -: the quality of being able to grasp and comprehend what is obscure : skill in discerning. : an act of perceiving or discerning something.

Concept note-2: -/dɪˈsɜː.nɪŋ/ showing good judgment, especially about style and quality: a discerning customer. SMART Vocabulary: related words and phrases. Wise and sensible.

Concept note-3: -characterized by or showing prejudicial treatment, especially as an indication of bias related to age, color, national origin, religion, sex, etc.: discriminatory practices in housing; a discriminatory tax.

Concept note-4: -Being discerning also often involves making a judgment, especially about things that aren’t obvious. For example, it takes discernment to accurately judge someone’s character.
