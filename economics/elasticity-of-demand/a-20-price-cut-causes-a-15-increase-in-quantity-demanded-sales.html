ELASTICITY OF DEMAND

[SOURCES]
a 20% price cut causes a 15% increase in quantity demanded (sales)

(A) PED =-1.33

(B) ** PED =-0.75

(C) PED =-7.5

(D) PED =-1.75

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If the price elasticity of demand is . 75 (i.e. 3/4), it means the % change in quantity demanded is lower or less than the % change in price (demand is inelastic or less than 1). In other words, quantity demanded is less responsive to changes in price for this good.

Concept note-2: -If the percent change in a goodâ€™s price is offset by an equal percent change in the quantity demanded, economists would label the demand for that good as unit elastic. So if a price of a good increases by 20 percent and the quantity demanded decreases by 20 percent, the demand for that good is considered unit elastic.

Concept note-3: -If a 15% increase in price for a good results in a 20% decrease in quantity demanded, the price elasticity of demand is: 0.75.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: We will use elasticity of demand equation to calculate the change in goods demanded. 10 % decrease in the price will result into 7.5 % change in goods demanded.
