If the price elasticity of demand is unit elastic how does quantity demanded change in response to a 25% increase in price?

(A) Quantity demanded doesnâ€™t change

(B) Quantity demanded decreases by 50%

(C) Quantity demanded increases by 50%

(D) Quantity demanded increases by 25%

(E) ** Quantity demanded decreases by 25%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: If demand is unit elastic, a 25 percent increase in price will result in 25 percent decrease in quantity demanded.

Concept note-2: -If the elasticity is equal to one, it means that the change in the quantity demanded is exactly equal to the change in price, so the demand response is exactly proportional to the change in price. We call this unitary elasticity, because unitary means one.

Concept note-3: -When percentage change in quantity demanded is equal to the percentage change in price, the elasticity of demand is unitary elastic.

Concept note-4: -We say that demand is elastic when quantity demanded changes a lot when the price changes; more precisely, the percentage change in quantity demanded is greater than the percentage change in price. In this case, quantity demanded is very responsive to changes in price.

Concept note-5: -Elastic demand or supply curves indicate that the quantity demanded or supplied responds to price changes in a greater than proportional manner. An inelastic demand or supply curve is one where a given percentage change in price will cause a smaller percentage change in quantity demanded or supplied.
