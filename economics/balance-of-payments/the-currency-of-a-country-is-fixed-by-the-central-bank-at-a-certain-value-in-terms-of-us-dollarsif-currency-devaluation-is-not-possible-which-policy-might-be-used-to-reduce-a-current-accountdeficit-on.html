BALANCE OF PAYMENTS

[SOURCES]
The currency of a country is fixed by the Central Bank at a certain value in terms of US dollars.If currency devaluation is not possible, which policy might be used to reduce a current accountdeficit on the balance of payments?(May/June 2012)

(A) A a decrease in interest rates

(B) B a decrease in tax rates

(C) C a decrease in tariffs on imports

(D) ** D a decrease in public expenditure

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -f) When value of domestic currency is tied to the value of another currency, it is known as ‘Pegging’. g) When value of a currency is fixed in terms of some other currency or in terms of gold, it is known as ‘Parity value’ of currency.

Concept note-2: -In general, when a currency loses value, people’s purchasing power declines as well because products-especially imported ones-cost more money. And when that causes a general rise in prices, it’s called inflation.

Concept note-3: -Devaluation causes a country’s exports to become less expensive, making them more competitive on the global market. This in turn means that imports are more expensive, making domestic consumers less likely to purchase them.

Concept note-4: -Understanding Devaluation Devaluation reduces the cost of a country’s exports, rendering them more competitive in the global market, which, in turn, increases the cost of imports. If imports are more expensive, domestic consumers are less likely to purchase them, further strengthening domestic businesses.
