On a graph with both a supply and demand curve, where are surpluses found?

(A) ** Above the equilibrium price.

(B) Below the equilibrium price.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The point where the demand and supply meet is the equilibrium price. The area above the supply level and below the equilibrium price is called product surplus (PS), and the area below the demand level and above the equilibrium price is the consumer surplus (CS).

Concept note-2: -Equilibrium: Where Supply and Demand Intersect On a graph, the point where the supply curve (S) and the demand curve (D) intersect is the equilibrium.

Concept note-3: -On a graph, the intersection of the demand and supply curves represents the equilibrium price.

Concept note-4: -The total economic surplus is represented on a graph by the intersection of the supply and demand curve. Quantity is represented on the x-axis, and price on the y-axis. The demand curve slopes down from a higher price to a lower quantity. The supply curve ascends from low price to high quantity.
