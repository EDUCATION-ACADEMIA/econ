Would there be a shortage or surplus of housing if the government puts a ceiling on the cost of low income housing?

(A) Surplus

(B) ** Shortage

(C) It would not affect the price

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A price ceiling above the competitive equilibrium price will result in a surplus. A price ceiling below the competitive equilibrium price will result in a shortage.

Concept note-2: -The ceiling price is binding and causes the equilibrium quantity to change – quantity demanded increases while quantity supplied decreases. It causes a quantity shortage of the amount Qd – Qs. In addition, a deadweight loss is created from the price ceiling.

Concept note-3: -Price ceilings prevent a price from rising above a certain level. When a price ceiling is set below the equilibrium price, quantity demanded will exceed quantity supplied, and excess demand or shortages will result. Price floors prevent a price from falling below a certain level.

Concept note-4: -In the case of a price ceiling, producer surplus decreases. (It is the triangle described by the area below p ¯ and above the supply curve.)
