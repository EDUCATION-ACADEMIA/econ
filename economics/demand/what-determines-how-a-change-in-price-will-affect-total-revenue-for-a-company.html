What determines how a change in price will affect total revenue for a company?

(A) ** Elasticity of Demand

(B) The company’s pricing policy

(C) Values of Elasticity

(D) The Consumer’s Incomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If price changes by a larger percentage than quantity demanded (i.e., if demand is price inelastic), total revenue will move in the direction of the price change. If price and quantity demanded change by the same percentage (i.e., if demand is unit price elastic), then total revenue does not change.

Concept note-2: -Elasticity of demand determines how a change in prices will affect a firm’s total revenue or income. A company’s total revenue is the amount of money the company receives by selling its goods. This is determined by the price of the goods and the quantity sold.

Concept note-3: -If the price of an elastic good increases, there is a corresponding quantity effect, where fewer units are sold, and therefore reducing revenue. The lower the price elasticity of demand, the less responsive the quantity demanded is given a change in price.

Concept note-4: -Price and total revenue have a negative relationship when demand is elastic (price elasticity > 1), which means that increases in price will lead to decreases in total revenue. Price changes will not affect total revenue when the demand is unit elastic (price elasticity = 1).

Concept note-5: -If demand is elastic, then a price increase reduces the total revenue. When the price increases, then the demand falls by a considerable percentage. Then, total revenue starts moving in contradictory directions. Therefore, total income declines when the price of any commodity rises.
