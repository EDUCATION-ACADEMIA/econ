The way that a change in price determines whether or not consumers buy goods

(A) elasticity of demand

(B) substitution effect

(C) ** law of demand

(D) complement

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In other words, the higher the price, the lower the level of demand. Because buyers have finite resources, their spending on a given product or commodity is limited as well, so higher prices reduce the quantity demanded. Conversely, demand rises as the product becomes more affordable.

Concept note-2: -The law of demand states that consumers buy more of a good when its price decreases and less when its price increases. The law of demand is the result of two separate behavior patterns that overlap, the substitution effect and the income effect.

Concept note-3: -The law of demand states that when prices rise, the quantity of demand falls. That also means that when prices drop, demand will grow. People base their purchasing decisions on price if all other things are equal. The exact quantity bought for each price level is described in the demand schedule.

Concept note-4: -The law of supply and demand is an economic theory that explains how supply and demand are related to each other and how that relationship affects the price of goods and services. Itâ€™s a fundamental economic principle that when supply exceeds demand for a good or service, prices fall.

Concept note-5: -The law of demand states that the quantity purchased varies inversely with price. In other words, the higher the price, the lower the quantity demanded. This occurs because of diminishing marginal utility.
