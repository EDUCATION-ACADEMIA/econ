What is the purpose of the European Union?

(A) to maintain the largest standing army in the world as a deterrent

(B) to enforce global peace treaties through military intervention

(C) to create a single, federal state covering the European continent

(D) ** to promote peace, security, and justice through cooperation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The treaty was designed to enhance European political and economic integration by creating a single currency (the euro), a unified foreign and security policy, and common citizenship rights and by advancing cooperation in the areas of immigration, asylum, and judicial affairs.

Concept note-2: -The EU continues to engage in crisis response, deploying military and civilian crisis management missions and operations to fragile environments. The EU has instruments for stabilisation and peacebuilding to help ensure sustainable peace.

Concept note-3: -The aims of the European Union within its borders are: promote peace, its values and the well-being of its citizens. offer freedom, security and justice without internal borders, while also taking appropriate measures at its external borders to regulate asylum and immigration and prevent and combat crime.

Concept note-4: -Development and cooperation Together, the EU institutions and countries are the world’s leading donor of development assistance and cooperation. The EU proposes legislation and policies to promote good governance and human and economic development, such as fighting hunger and preserving natural resources.

Concept note-5: -It sets out EU objectives, rules for EU institutions, how decisions are made and the relationship between the EU and its member countries. Treaties are amended to make the EU more efficient and transparent, to prepare for new member countries and to introduce new areas of cooperation – such as the single currency.
