Which equation can be used to calculate the “real value” of a $5000 investment with an interest rate of 5.2% p.a. compounded quarterly for 5 years, the average rate of inflation was 1.9% over this period. [1 pt]

(A) U n = 5000 × ( 1 + 0 . 052 ) 5 ( 1 + 0 . 019 ) 5

(B) U n = 5000 × ( 1 + 0 . 052 ) 20 ( 1 + 0 . 019 ) 5

(C) U n = 5000 × ( 1 + 0 . 013 ) 20 ( 1 + 0 . 019 ) 20

(D) ** U n = 5000 × ( 1 + 0 . 013 ) 20 ( 1 + 0 . 019 ) 5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The real rate of return formula is: (1+NominalRate) ÷ (1+InflationRate)-1. This calculation determines the cash value of your investment after accounting for the impact of inflation and taxes.

Concept note-2: -An investment of $1, 000 made today will be worth $1, 480.24 in five years at interest rate of 8% compounded semi-annually.

Concept note-3: -Summary: An investment of $10000 today invested at 6% for five years at simple interest will be $13, 000.

Concept note-4: -The future value of $1, 000 one year from now invested at 5% is $1, 050, and the present value of $1, 050 one year from now assuming 5% interest is earned is $1, 000.
