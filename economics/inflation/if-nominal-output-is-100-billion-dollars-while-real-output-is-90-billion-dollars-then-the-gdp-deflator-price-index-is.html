If nominal output is $100 billion dollars while real output is $90 billion dollars then the GDP deflator (Price Index) is

(A) approximately 100

(B) approximately 110

(C) ** approximately 111.1

(D) approximately-110

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -GDP deflator is a measure of price level in an economy and is measured as a ratio of nominal to real GDP. This means that GDP deflator is calculated as nominal GDP divided by real GDP multiplied by 100.

Concept note-2: -If the GDP deflator is greater than 100, then the economy has seen a positive change in price level since the base year. In other words, the economy has experienced inflation.

Concept note-3: -Nominal GDP = Real GDP x GDP Deflator GDP Deflator: A measurement of the change in price over a duration of time (inflation or deflation). It is calculated as the ratio of Nominal GDP to Real GDP.
