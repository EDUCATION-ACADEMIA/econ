How many ways does Georgia have to transport produced goods throughout the State?

(A) 1

(B) 2

(C) 3

(D) ** 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The interaction of the four transportation systems is essential to the state’s economy. Georgia, being the “transportation hub” of the southeast, transports people and products quickly and efficiently via air, road, railway, and sea to national and global markets, helping businesses save time and money.

Concept note-2: -In addition to 20, 000 miles of federal and state highways, including two major transcontinental highways, Georgia hosts two Class 1 railroads and 24 short lines that together move almost 200 million tons of cargo over 5, 000 miles of rail each year.

Concept note-3: -Georgia’s four systems of transportation are integral to the state’s economy by transporting people and products quickly and efficiently, providing job opportunities, and play a crucial role in the economic development of many cities.

Concept note-4: -The interstate highway system is essential to Georgia’s economic success. Georgia boasts highways that facilitate speed and reliability for shipments to the rest of the United States and the world.

Concept note-5: -Georgia’s 1, 253 miles of interstate highways perform several functions vital to the state’s economy: connecting Georgia to the rest of the nation, linking the state’s major cities, and helping move suburban commuters to and from work centers.
