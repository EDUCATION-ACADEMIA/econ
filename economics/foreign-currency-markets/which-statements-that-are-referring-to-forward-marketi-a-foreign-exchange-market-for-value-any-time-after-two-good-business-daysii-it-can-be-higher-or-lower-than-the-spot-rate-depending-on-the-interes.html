(A) ** i and ii

(C) iii and iv

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What Is a Forward Market? A forward market is an over-the-counter marketplace that sets the price of a financial instrument or asset for future delivery. Forward markets are used for trading a range of instruments, but the term is primarily used with reference to the foreign exchange market.

Concept note-2: -The forward market is the marketplace that sets the price of assets and financial instruments (Bonds, Swaps, Equity, Cap, Futures, Forward rate agreements, Bills of exchange, and so on) for future delivery and is used for financial instrument trading.

Concept note-3: -C is correct. An agreement to convert one currency into another in the future is a foreign exchange transaction that involves the forward market.

Concept note-4: -7.8 Forward exchange contract means an agreement to exchange different currencies at a forward rate. 7.9 Forward rate is the specified exchange rate for exchange of two currencies at a specified future date.
