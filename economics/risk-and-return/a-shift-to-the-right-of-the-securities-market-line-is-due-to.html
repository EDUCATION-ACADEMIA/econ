RISK AND RETURN

[SOURCES]
A shift to the right of the Securities Market Line is due to

(A) risk aversion

(B) ** Inflationary Expectation

(C) risk aggressiveness

(D) pure expectation theory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Shifts in the SML Several different exogenous variables can impact the slope of the security market line. For example, the real interest rate in the economy might change; inflation may pick up or slow down; or a recession can occur and investors become generally more risk-averse.

Concept note-2: -The major forces causing shifts in the SML are changes in risk appetite (i.e., investors’ willingness to accept risk), inflation, and interest rates.

Concept note-3: -Security Market Line Assumptions The larger the level of systematic risk, the larger the expected return for the security is – more risk equals more reward. It is a linear relationship and explains why the security market line is a straight line.
