Treasury Bonds

(A) Bonds with no maturities but interest issued by the US treasury

(B) ** Interest bearing obligations issued by the US Treasury with maturities

(C) Obligations issued by the US Treasury

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Treasury Notes are government securities which are issued with maturities of 2, 3, 5, 7, and 10 years. Notes pay interest every six months.

Concept note-2: -There are five types of Treasury marketable securities: Bills, Notes, TIPS, Floating Rate Notes and Bonds.

Concept note-3: -Treasury bonds (T-Bonds) are long-term bonds having a maturity between 10 to 30 years. T-Bonds give interest or coupon payments semi-annually and have $1, 000 face values. The bonds help to offset shortfalls in the federal budget.

Concept note-4: -When a Treasury bond matures – meaning it has reached its maturity date and expires – the investor is paid out the full face value of the T-bond. That means if the bondholder holds a Treasury bond worth $10, 000, he or she will receive the $10, 000 principal back, as well as earning interest on the investment.
