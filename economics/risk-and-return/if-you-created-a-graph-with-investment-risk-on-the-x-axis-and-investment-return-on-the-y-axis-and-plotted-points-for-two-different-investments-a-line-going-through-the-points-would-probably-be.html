RISK AND RETURN

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

If you created a graph with investment risk on the x-axis and investment return on the y-axis and plotted points for two different investments, a line going through the points would probably be:

(A) ** Positively sloped

(B) Negatively sloped

(C) Have a slope of zero

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In general, investments that are riskier tend to provide higher returns over time than investments with less risk. The correct answer is a: The stock and bond markets tend to reward risk-taking over the long term. This is called the risk-reward tradeoff.

Concept note-2: -High-risk investments may offer the chance of higher returns than other investments might produce, but they put your money at higher risk. This means that if things go well, high-risk investments can produce high returns. But if things go badly, you could lose all of the money you invested.

Concept note-3: -The potential return of any investment should be directly related to the risk that the investor assumes. During inflationary times, there is a risk that the financial return on an investment will not keep pace with the rate of inflation.

Concept note-4: -Vanguard Small-Cap (VB): Historical Returns. In the last 15 Years, the Vanguard Small-Cap (VB) ETF obtained a 9.61% compound annual return, with a 20.57% standard deviation. In 2022, the ETF granted a 1.30% dividend yield.
