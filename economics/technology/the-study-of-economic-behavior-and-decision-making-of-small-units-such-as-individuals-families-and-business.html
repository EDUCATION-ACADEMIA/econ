EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Economics: the study of how individuals, families, businesses, and societies use limited resources to fulfill their unlimited wants.

Concept note-2: -Microeconomics is based on models of consumers or firms (which economists call agents) that make decisions about what to buy, sell, or produce-with the assumption that those decisions result in perfect market clearing (demand equals supply) and other ideal conditions.

Concept note-3: -Behavioral Economics is the study of psychology as it relates to the economic decision-making processes of individuals and institutions.

Concept note-4: -Microeconomics is a branch of mainstream economics that studies the behavior of individuals and firms in making decisions regarding the allocation of scarce resources and the interactions among these individuals and firms.
