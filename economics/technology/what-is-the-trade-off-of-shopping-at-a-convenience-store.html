What is the trade-off of shopping at a convenience store?

(A) ** Limited variety and higher prices

(B) Large variety of brands and higher prices

(C) Lots of choices of sizes and brands and cheaper prices

(D) Smaller selection and cheaper prices

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Having such a store near you always comes in handy. Because it is open 24 hours a day, seven days a week, one can get ahold of anything he needs in a flash. Convenience stores are found almost anywhere. You do not have to go too far or wait till morning to buy what you need.

Concept note-2: -Ross Stores (Ross Dress for Less), T.J. Maxx (USA), T.K. Maxx (UK, Germany, Australia), and Burlington are examples of independent off-price retailers. Ross Dress for Less® is an off-price retailer with 1, 480 locations in the United States.

Concept note-3: -A convenience store, bodega, convenience shop, corner store or corner shop is a small retail business that stocks a range of everyday items such as coffee, groceries, snack foods, confectionery, soft drinks, ice creams, tobacco products, lottery tickets, over-the-counter drugs, toiletries, newspapers and magazines.

Concept note-4: -Itinerant Retailing. Fixed shop Retailing.
