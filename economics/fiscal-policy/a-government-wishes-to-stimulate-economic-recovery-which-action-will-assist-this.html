FISCAL POLICY

[SOURCES]
A government wishes to stimulate economic recovery. Which action will assist this?

(A) decreasing government investment

(B) ** decreasing income tax

(C) increasing indirect taxation

(D) increasing interest rates

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Policy tools for stimulating the economy include interest rate cuts, government spending increases, and quantitative easing. Policymakers generally direct stimulus programs toward key economic sectors to take advantage of multiplier effects that they hope will indirectly increase private sector spending.

Concept note-2: -In the short term, governments may focus on macroeconomic stabilization-for example, expanding spending or cutting taxes to stimulate an ailing economy, or slashing spending or raising taxes to combat rising inflation or to help reduce external vulnerabilities.

Concept note-3: -Increasing the aggregate (total) consumption is the best way of promoting economic growth. Basically, augmented consumption triggers an increased demand for goods and services, which translates to a rise in production as well as investments as producers strive to satisfy the prevailing demand.

Concept note-4: -During a recession, the government may lower tax rates or increase spending to encourage demand and spur economic activity. Conversely, to combat inflation, it may raise rates or cut spending to cool down the economy.
