Kennedy won money in a radio contest 10 years ago. She put this money in a bank account that earned 2.9% interest compounded quarterly . She now has $4005.09 in the account. How much money did she originally win? (Hint:You are finding the value for P)

(A) $4915.59

(B) $5346.92

(C) $2579.81

(D) ** $3000.00

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The amount that she earned in 10 years is $4, 005.09. Given that, Kennedy won $3, 000 from a radio contest.

Concept note-2: -488.86. Hence, Compound interest would be Rs. 488.86.

Concept note-3: -Compound interest, can be calculated using the formula FV = P*(1+R/N)^(N*T), where FV is the future value of the loan or investment, P is the initial principal amount, R is the annual interest rate, N represents the number of times interest is compounded per year, and T represents time in years.

Concept note-4: -The formula to convert simple interest to compound annual interest is (1 + R/N)N-1, where R is the simple interest rate, and N equals the number of times interest is compounded in a year.
