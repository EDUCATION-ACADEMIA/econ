Quarterly means how many times a year?

(A) ** 4

(B) 2

(C) 1

(D) 6

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As its name suggests, there are four quarterly periods in a year, meaning a publicly-traded company would issue four quarterly reports per year.

Concept note-2: -A quarterly event happens four times a year, at intervals of three months.

Concept note-3: -The traditional calendar quarters that make up the year are: Dates for Q1: January 1 – March 31. Dates for Q2: April 1 – June 3. Dates for Q3: July 1 – September 30. Dates for Q4: October 1 – December 31.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: There are 20 quarterly periods in 5 years. When a compound interest account has quarterly compounding periods, the interest is compounded four times each year; once at the end of each quarter. That is, quarterly periods in a year refer to the four quarters of a year.
