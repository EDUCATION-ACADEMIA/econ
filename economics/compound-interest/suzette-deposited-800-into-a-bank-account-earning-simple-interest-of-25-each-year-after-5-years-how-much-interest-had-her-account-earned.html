COMPOUND INTEREST

[SOURCES]
Suzette deposited $800 into a bank account earning simple interest of 2.5% each year. After 5 years, how much interest had her account earned?

(A) ** $100

(B) $900

(C) $1000

(D) $1800

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -5% = 0.05 . Then multiply the original amount by the interest rate. $1, 000 * 0.05 = $50 . That’s it.

Concept note-2: -Hence, one can double his or her money in 20 years at a 5% simple interest rate.

Concept note-3: -Simple Interest Formula Thus, if simple interest is charged at 5% on a $10, 000 loan that is taken out for three years, then the total amount of interest payable by the borrower is calculated as $10, 000 x 0.05 x 3 = $1, 500.

Concept note-4: -Hence, Compound interest will be 51.25 Rs.
