[SOURCES]
Fee charged by the Federal Reserve Bank for member banks to borrow money from the FED (hint:@ a reduced rate)?

(A) inflation

(B) interest

(C) ** discount rate

(D) reserve requirement

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Federal Open Markets Committee (FOMC) sets the federal funds rate-also known as the federal funds target rate or the fed funds rate-to guide overnight lending among U.S. banks. It’s set as a range between an upper and lower limit. The federal funds rate is currently 4.50% to 4.75%.

Concept note-2: -The discount rate is the interest rate charged by the Fed for loans it makes through the Fed’s discount window. Because banks will not likely borrow at a higher rate than they can borrow from the Fed, the discount rate acts as a ceiling for the federal funds rate.

Concept note-3: -When a member bank borrows reserves from the Fed, it pays an interest rate called the discount rate. buys or sells previously issued government bonds.
