An economy has achieved productive efficiency if:

(A) it is self-sufficient in all products.

(B) it has eliminated all negative externalities.

(C) cost equals price in all its industries

(D) ** it is unable to produce more of one good without producing less of another.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To be productively efficient means the economy must be producing on its production possibility frontier. (i.e. it is impossible to produce more of one good without producing less of another).

Concept note-2: -Production efficiency is an economic term describing a level at which an economy or entity can no longer produce additional amounts of a good without lowering the production level of another product. This happens when production is reportedly occurring along a production possibility frontier (PPF).

Concept note-3: -Productive efficiency is a point at which the economy or a business entity can produce the maximum quantity of goods. This is achieved by strategic allocation of finite resources-capital, labor, equipment, material, technology, and energy.

Concept note-4: -Productive efficiency is a situation where firms seek the best combination of inputs to lower their costs of production. Allocative efficiency means that economic resources are distributed in a way that produces the highest consumer satisfaction relative to the cost of inputs.

Concept note-5: -Productive efficiency occurs when a firm is combining resources in such a way as to produce a given output at the lowest possible average total cost. Costs will be minimised at the lowest point on a firmâ€™s short run average total cost curve.
