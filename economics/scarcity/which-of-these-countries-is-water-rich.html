Which of these countries is “water rich”?

(A) Australia

(B) Egypt

(C) ** New Zealand

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Compared with many other countries New Zealand is relatively water-rich.

Concept note-2: -Brazil is in the top position having the most amount of renewable water resources of 8, 233 km3, following by Russia, US, Canada and China.

Concept note-3: -New Zealand has a relative abundance of water in its water cycle, coming from water evaporated from its surrounding major oceans then falling as precipitation in its hilly or mountainous interior. This then flows out to the lower land and the coasts in the rivers above ground and in the aquifers below.

Concept note-4: -Around ⅓ of the water that New Zealand uses comes from groundwater aquifers. These aquifers are estimated to store 612 billion cubic meters of water. These aquifers provide high-quality drinking water.

Concept note-5: -Dams and reservoirs can run low, especially in late summer, but generally water is freely available. Local councils administer the water supply and will let you know about any restrictions.
