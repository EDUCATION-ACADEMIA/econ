SCARCITY

[SOURCES]
In many countries worldwide, women and children are responsible for collecting water for the family.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In this case, the Supreme Court stated that “state is responsible for providing clean drinking water to the citizens.” In India many laws have been enacted regarding water and water-based resources.

Concept note-2: -In most societies, women have the prime responsibility for the management of household water supply, sanitation and health. The provision of hygiene and sanitation are often considered women’s tasks.

Concept note-3: -The United Nations recognizes the right to safe and clean drinking water and sanitation as a human right. But one third of people worldwide still lack access to safe drinking water and 3 billion people do not have hand-washing facilities.

Concept note-4: -Water scarcity takes a greater toll on women and children because they are often the ones responsible for collecting it.
