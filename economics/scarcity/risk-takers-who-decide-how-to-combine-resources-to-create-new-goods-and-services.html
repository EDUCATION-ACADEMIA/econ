[SOURCES]
Risk takers who decide how to combine resources to create new goods and services.

(A) Business Leaders

(B) ** Entrepreneurs

(C) Tycoons

(D) Industry Managers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A person who undertakes the risk of starting a new business venture is called an entrepreneur. An entrepreneur creates a firm to realize their idea, known as entrepreneurship, which aggregates capital and labor in order to produce goods or services for profit.

Concept note-2: -Risk-taking is an important part of entrepreneurship and business. Risk-taking is defined as trying out something new when the results are largely unknown. A risk-taker is someone who will risk everything in the hope of achieving their goals.

Concept note-3: -Entrepreneurs are people who take the risks of organizing productive resources to make goods and services. Profit is an important incentive that leads entrepreneurs to accept the risks of business failure.

Concept note-4: -An entrepreneur is an individual who starts and runs a business with limited resources and planning, and is responsible for all the risks and rewards of their business venture. The business idea usually encompasses a new product or service rather than an existing business model.

Concept note-5: -Schumpeter stressed the role of the entrepreneur as an innovator who implements change in an economy by introducing new goods or new methods of production. In the Schumpeterian view, the entrepreneur is a disruptive force in an economy.
