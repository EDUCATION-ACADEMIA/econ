A gallon of water weighs 8.34 pounds.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Question: How much does a gallon of water weigh? Answer: A US gallon of water weighs 8.34 lbs or 3.78 kg at 62 °F (17 °C). An imperial gallon (UK) weighs 10.022 lbs or 4.546 kg, at its most dense temperature, which is 2.20456 lbs / L at 4 °C or 39 °F.

Concept note-2: -Wondering how much a gallon of water weighs? A US gallon is equal to 8.34 pounds or 3.78 kilos when measured at 62°F (or 17°C). So there you have it – now you know the answer!

Concept note-3: -What is the conversion for 1 gallon to pounds? If you are dealing with water and need to convert 1 gallon to pounds, a gallon of pure water at 39.2 °F (4.0 °C) weighs 8.35 lb.

Concept note-4: -1 gallon of water weighs 8.3454 lbs (3.7854 kg). But REMEMBER the important caveat about temperature above! 2 gallons of water weighs 16.69 lbs (7.57 kg)

Concept note-5: -Answer: 16 ounces (oz) of water is equal to 1 pound in weight. Let’s understand the relationship and the conversion between pounds and ounces.
