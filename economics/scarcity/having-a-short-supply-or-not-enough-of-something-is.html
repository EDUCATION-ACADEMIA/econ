ECONOMICS

SCARCITY

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

Having a short supply or not enough of something is:

(A) supply

(B) demand

(C) ** scarcity

(D) trade-off

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Scarcity is one of the key concepts of economics. It means that the demand for a good or service is greater than the availability of the good or service. Therefore, scarcity can limit the choices available to the consumers who ultimately make up the economy.

Concept note-2: -Lesson Summary. Scarcity and shortage are two fundamental concepts. Scarcity refers to the existence of limited resources that are not enough to address unlimited human needs or demands. On the other hand, shortage refers to an occurrence whereby the order in the market outdoes the supply available at a given time.

Concept note-3: -The scarcity principle is an economic theory in which a limited supply of a good-coupled with a high demand for that good-results in a mismatch between the desired supply and demand equilibrium.

Concept note-4: -The resources that we value-time, money, labor, tools, land, and raw materials-exist in limited supply. There are simply never enough resources to meet all our needs and desires. This condition is known as scarcity.

Concept note-5: -Scarcity falls into three distinctive categories: demand-induced, supply-induced, and structural.
