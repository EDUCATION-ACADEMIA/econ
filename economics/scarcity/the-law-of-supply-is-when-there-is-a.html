The law of supply is when there is a

(A) Rise in price and a decrease in quantity supplied

(B) ** increase in price and an increase in quantity supplied

(C) increase in price and an increase in supply

(D) decrease in price and a decrease in supply

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What Is the Law of Supply? The law of supply is the microeconomic law that states that, all other factors being equal, as the price of a good or service increases, the quantity of goods or services that suppliers offer will increase, and vice versa.

Concept note-2: -The law of supply states that there is a direct relationship between price and quantity supplied. In other words, when the price increases the quantity supplied also increases.

Concept note-3: -An increase in supply, all other things unchanged, will cause the equilibrium price to fall; quantity demanded will increase. A decrease in supply will cause the equilibrium price to rise; quantity demanded will decrease.

Concept note-4: -The law of supply states that an increase in the price of goods or services results in an increase in the quantity that suppliers make available to the market. Existing suppliers increase production of higher-priced goods to maximize profits, while new suppliers may also enter the market.

Concept note-5: -The law of supply says that when prices rise, companies see more profit potential and increase the supply of goods and services. The law of demand states that as prices rise, customers buy less. Theoretically, a free market will move toward an equilibrium quantity and price where supply and demand intersect.
