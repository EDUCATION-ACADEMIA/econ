Concept note-1: -In 2005, Congress passed the Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention and Consumer Protection Act (BAPCPA) after heated debate. The new law was designed to deter people from pursuing bankruptcy by making filing for it more difficult and expensive, as well as less financially advantageous.

Concept note-2: -Types of debt that cannot be discharged in bankruptcy include alimony, child support, and certain unpaid taxes. Other types of debt that cannot be alleviated in bankruptcy include debts for willful and malicious injury to another person or property.

Concept note-3: -A Chapter 7 bankruptcy is typically removed from your credit report 10 years after the date you filed, and this is done automatically, so you don’t have to initiate that removal.

Concept note-4: -Make sure Chapter 13 is the right choice. Analyze your debt. Value your property. Gauge your income. Fill out the bankruptcy forms. Take the required pre-filing course.
