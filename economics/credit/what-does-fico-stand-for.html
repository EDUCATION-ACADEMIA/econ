What does FICO stand for?

(A) First Intern Corporation

(B) Foreign Investment Corporation

(C) Future Interest Corporation

(D) ** Fair Isaac Corporation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -FICO is the acronym for Fair Isaac Corporation, as well as the name for the credit scoring model that Fair Isaac Corporation developed. A FICO credit score is a tool used by many lenders to determine if a person qualifies for a credit card, mortgage, or other loan.

Concept note-2: -FICO is an acronym for Fair Isaac Corporation, the company that developed the FICO® credit scoring models that many lenders use to help accurately predict a consumer’s ability to repay a debt on time.

Concept note-3: -Originally called Fair, Isaac and Company (hence the abbreviation FICO), this name was changed to Fair Isaac Corporation in 2003.

Concept note-4: -The company provides analytics software and tools used across multiple industries to manage risk, fight fraud, build more profitable customer relationships, optimize operations and meet strict government regulations.

Concept note-5: -FICO was created in 1956 by engineer Bill Fair and mathematician Earl Isaac, and its name comes from what was Fair, Isaac, and Company. The initial goal was to improve business decisions by using data intelligently. Fair and Isaac developed and sold their first credit scoring system in 1958.
