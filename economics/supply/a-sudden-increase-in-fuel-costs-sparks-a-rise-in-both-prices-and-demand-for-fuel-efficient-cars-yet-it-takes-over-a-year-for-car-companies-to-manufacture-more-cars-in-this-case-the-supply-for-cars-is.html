SUPPLY

[SOURCES]
A sudden increase in fuel costs sparks a rise in both prices and demand for fuel-efficient cars. Yet it takes over a year for car companies to manufacture more cars. In this case, the supply for cars is

(A) ** inelastic.

(B) elastic.

(C) static.

(D) inferior.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A market supply schedule shows the relationship between prices and the total quantity supplied by all firms in a particular market.

Concept note-2: -Any change in the cost of an input used to produce a good-such as raw materials, machinery, or labor-will affect supply. A rise in the cost of an input will cause a fall in supply at all price levels because the good has become more expensive to produce.

Concept note-3: -Total product costs can be determined by adding together the total direct materials and labor costs as well as the total manufacturing overhead costs.

Concept note-4: -The law of diminishing returns refers to increasing one input in a production process while other inputs remain constant. As each new unit of the increasing input is added, the marginal output gets smaller.
