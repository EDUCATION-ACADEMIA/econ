The benefits of a common currency, such as the euro used by the EU, include:

(A) ** Increased stability for exporters, importers, tourists, investors, and creditors.

(B) Increased returns on investment and speculation on foreign currency markets.

(C) Less incentive for employees to leave to work in other countries

(D) All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -However, it cannot be denied that the use of a common currency facilitates the exchange, specialization, and integration of national markets into a wider market. It also significantly reduces the costs of transaction induced by currency risk and increases benefits from international trade.

Concept note-2: -Currently, the euro (€) is the official currency of 20 out of 27 EU member countries which together constitute the Eurozone, officially called the euro area.

Concept note-3: -The euro was an attempt to advance the economic integration of Europe by having the countries of the eurozone share a common currency.
