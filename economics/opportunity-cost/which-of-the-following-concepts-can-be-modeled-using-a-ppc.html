Which of the following concepts can be modeled using a PPC?

(A) Societies have preferences about what should be produced

(B) Governments, rather than markets, are more efficient allocators of resources

(C) Prices that the goods will sell for will be determined in a market

(D) A society can produce more of any good just by reallocating its resources more efficiently

(E) ** When all resources are being used, making more of one good means making less of the other good

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Production Possibilities Curve (PPC) is a model used to show the tradeoffs associated with allocating resources between the production of two goods. The PPC can be used to illustrate the concepts of scarcity, opportunity cost, efficiency, inefficiency, economic growth, and contractions.

Concept note-2: -The production possibilities curve illustrates the maximum possible output for two products when there are limited resources.

Concept note-3: -When all resources are being used, making more of one good means making less of the other good. The downward slope of the curve represents tradeoffs. Producing more of one of the goods will require shifting resources away from one good and toward the other good. A production possibilities curve.

Concept note-4: -In economics, the production possibilities curve is a visualization that demonstrates the most efficient production of a pair of goods. Each point on the curve shows how much of each good will be produced when resources shift to making more of one good and less of another.

Concept note-5: -The resources are given and remain constant. The technology used in the production process remains constant. The resources and technology are fully and efficiently utilized. The technique of production remains constant.
