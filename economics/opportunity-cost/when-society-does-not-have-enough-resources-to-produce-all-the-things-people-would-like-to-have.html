OPPORTUNITY COST

[SOURCES]
When society does not have enough resources to produce all the things people would like to have.

(A) ** Scarcity

(B) Economic Growth

(C) Paradox of Value

(D) Want

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Scarcity– the fundamental problem facing all societies. It is the condition that results from society not having enough resources to produce all the things that people would like to have.

Concept note-2: -One of the defining features of economics is scarcity, which deals with how people satisfy unlimited wants and needs with limited resources. Scarcity affects the monetary value people place on goods and services and how governments and private firms decide to distribute resources.

Concept note-3: -Scarce Goods and Services Almost every good we consume as individuals, or as a society, costs something and is scarce. By consuming one good, another good is foregone. Therefore, scarcity creates a need for decisions and trade-offs to be made.

Concept note-4: -Productive Efficiency. Allocative Efficiency. Full Employment, and. Equity.
