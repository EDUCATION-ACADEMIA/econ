WHAT ARE THE 3 COMPONENTS OF AN ATTITUDE?

(A) ** COGNITIVE, AFFECTIVE AND CONATIVE

(B) BEHAVIOURAL, PERSONALITY AND MOTIVATION

(C) LEARNING, BELIEF AND LIFESTYLE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cognitive-The cognitive component refers to a person’s knowledge or belief about something. Affective-The affective component refers to a person’s emotional response to something. Conative-The conative component refers to actions taken. 17-Apr-2022

Concept note-2: -Attitudes have three components: Cognitive, affective, and behavioral intentions. The cognitive component of an attitude is a statement of belief about something.

Concept note-3: -Attitudes can include up to three components: cognitive, emotional, and behavioral.

Concept note-4: -Cognitive consists strategy, belief and creative thinking. Affective focus on positive emotion and attitude while conative are based on desire, intention, initiative and willingness. Strategy means ways to design and implement while belief refer to trust of an individual to something.

Concept note-5: -Cognitive component of attitude refers to the thought, perception or ideas of the person toward the object of the attitude. Affective component is about emotional reaction or feeling of the person toward the object of the attitude such as like or dislike.
