ECONOMIC SYSTEMS

[SOURCES]
What are the 3 basic economic questions that all economic systems must answer?

(A) ** What to produce, how to produce it, and for whom to produce the goods

(B) When to produce, why to produce, and how long to produce

(C) to what extent to produce, how much to produce, why to produce

(D) what to produce, why to produce, and how much to produce

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Economists address these three questions: (1) What goods and services should be produced to meet consumer needs? (2) How should they be produced, and who should produce them? (3) Who should receive goods and services? The answers to these questions depend on a country’s economic system.

Concept note-2: -An economic system is any system of allocating scarce resources. Economic systems answer three basic questions: what will be produced, how will it be produced, and how will the output society produces be distributed?

Concept note-3: -The three basic decisions made by all economies are what to produce, how it is produced, and who consumes it. Scarce resources have a variety of possible uses (such as natural materials or labor), and economies must determine what those raw resources should be used for.

Concept note-4: -There are three main types of economic systems: command, market, and mixed. We will briefly describe each of these three types.

Concept note-5: -What to produce? ➢ What should be produced in a world with limited resources? How to produce? ➢ What resources should be used? Who consumes what is produced? ➢ Who acquires the product?
