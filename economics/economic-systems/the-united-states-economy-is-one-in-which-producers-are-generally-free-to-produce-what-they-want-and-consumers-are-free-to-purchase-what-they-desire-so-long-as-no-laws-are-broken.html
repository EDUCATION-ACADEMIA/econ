ECONOMIC SYSTEMS

[SOURCES]
The United States economy is one in which producers are generally free to produce what they want, and consumers are free to purchase what they desire, so long as no laws are broken.

(A) Market Economy

(B) Command Economy

(C) Liberated Economy

(D) ** Mixed Market Economy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In capitalist economic systems, such as that of the United States, consumers and producers individually determine which goods and services to produce and which to purchase.

Concept note-2: -The U.S. economy is essentially a free market economy-an economic market that is run by supply and demand-with some government regulation.

Concept note-3: -There are many different types of economic systems used throughout the world. Some examples are socialism, communism, and capitalism. The United States has a capitalistic system.

Concept note-4: -A market economy has freedom of choice and free enterprise. Private entrepreneurs are free to get and use resources and use them to produce goods and services.

Concept note-5: -Describe some of the economic choices people and producers in the United States are free to make. People choose their own occupations, employers, and uses for their money; producers choose where/how they produce.
