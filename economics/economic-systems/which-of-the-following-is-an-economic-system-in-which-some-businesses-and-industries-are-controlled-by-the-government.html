Which of the following is an economic system in which some businesses and industries are controlled by the government?

(A) Free Enterprise

(B) Communist

(C) ** Socialist

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Socialist Economy is an economic system where the factors of production like labour, natural resources or capital goods are under the control of the government.

Concept note-2: -In a pure command economy, there is no private sector, as the central government owns or controls all business. In a command economy, government officials set national economic priorities, including how and when to generate economic growth, how to allocate resources, and how to distribute the output.

Concept note-3: -What Is Socialism? Socialism is a populist economic and political system based on collective, common, or public ownership of the means of production. Those means of production include the machinery, tools, and factories used to produce goods that aim to directly satisfy human needs.

Concept note-4: -A command economy is an economic system where the government has control over the production and pricing of goods and services.

Concept note-5: -Socialism. The features of socialism are the opposite of those just listed for capitalism and were spelled out most famously by Karl Marx. Socialism is an economic system in which the means of production are collectively owned, usually by the government.
