ECONOMIC SYSTEMS

[SOURCES]
Resources that can’t be replaced like oil, gold, and silver. Once they are taken out of the ground, they can’t grow back or be replaced.

(A) Human Resources

(B) Renewable Resources

(C) ** Nonrenewable Resources

(D) Natural Resources

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nonrenewable energy resources include coal, natural gas, oil, and nuclear energy. Once these resources are used up, they cannot be replaced, which is a major problem for humanity as we are currently dependent on them to supply most of our energy needs.

Concept note-2: -Renewable energy resources include solar, water, wind, biomass, and geothermal power. These resources are usually replaced at the same rate that we use them. Scientists know that the Sun will continue to shine for billions of years. So we can use the solar energy without it ever running out.

Concept note-3: -Fossil energy sources, including oil, coal and natural gas, are non-renewable resources that formed when prehistoric plants and animals died and were gradually buried by layers of rock.

Concept note-4: -The main alternatives to oil and gas energy include nuclear power, solar power, ethanol, and wind power.

Concept note-5: -Why is silver valuable? Silver is valuable because of its rarity as a precious metal and non-renewable resource coupled with its many uses.
