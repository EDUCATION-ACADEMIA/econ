[SOURCES]
The Economist, Freidrich von Hayek, thought

(A) Competition is a regulatory force in the economy.

(B) Government intervention is necessary for stability.

(C) Government should not control the money supply.

(D) ** Less government intervention gives people more economic freedom.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“While Hayek did believe that government intervention in the price system could take us from free-market governance toward totalitarianism, he also endorsed an extensive system of social services, ” Angner explains. In fact, Hayek advocated a limited system of wealth redistribution.

Concept note-2: -Why was Friedrich von Hayek against government intervention in an economy? A. It would reduce people’s economic freedom.

Concept note-3: -Hayek’s theory posits the natural interest rate as an intertemporal price; that is, a price that coordinates the decisions of savers and investors through time. The cycle occurs when the market rate of interest (that is, the one prevailing in the market) diverges from this natural rate of interest.

Concept note-4: -The major problem for any economy, he argued, is how people’s actions are coordinated. He noticed, as Adam Smith had, that the price system-free markets-did a remarkable job of coordinating people’s actions, even though that coordination was not part of anyone’s intent. The market, said Hayek, was a spontaneous order.
