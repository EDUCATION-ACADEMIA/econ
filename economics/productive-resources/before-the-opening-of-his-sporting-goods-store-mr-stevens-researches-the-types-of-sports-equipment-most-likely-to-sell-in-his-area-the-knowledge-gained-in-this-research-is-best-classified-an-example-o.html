Before the opening of his sporting goods store, Mr. Stevens researches the types of sports equipment most likely to sell in his area. The knowledge gained in this research is BEST classified an example of

(A) labor.

(B) ** human capital.

(C) physical capital.

(D) allocation of resources.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The answer is human capital as this is the one responsible of affecting the perform labor in ways to produce an economic value with the use of knowledge, attributes, habits and even creativity in which Mr. Stevens demonstrates as he research in his benefit of having an economic value in his good store.

Concept note-2: -One of the defining features of economics is scarcity, which deals with how people satisfy unlimited wants and needs with limited resources. Scarcity affects the monetary value people place on goods and services and how governments and private firms decide to distribute resources.

Concept note-3: -The categories of goods used by ordinary people thatâ€™s affected the most by scarcity is nondurable goods and consumer goods. Scarcity could arise if certain capital goods stopped being produced or they were broken which would prevent the manufacturer from developing products with that item.

Concept note-4: -Human Capital-Key takeaways In Economics, human capital refers to the level of health, education, training, and skill of workers. Human capital is one of the primary determinants of the productivity and efficiency of labor, which is one of the four main factors of production.

Concept note-5: -The correct answer is (iv) every choice involves an opportunity cost. Because resources are not unlimited, whenever we make a choice, we let another choice go away. This is implied by scarcity, i.e., the fact that we cannot have it all.
