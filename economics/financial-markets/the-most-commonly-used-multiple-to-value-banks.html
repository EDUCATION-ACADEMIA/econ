The most commonly used multiple to value banks:

(A) ** Price to Book

(B) Price to Earnings

(C) EV to EBITDA

(D) Price to Sales

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The average P/B ratio for banking firms, as of the first quarter of 2021, is approximately 1.28. P/B is sometimes calculated as an absolute value, dividing a company’s total market capitalization by the book value from the company’s current balance sheet. The calculation is sometimes done on a per-share basis.

Concept note-2: -Valuation Multiples – Banks and Insurance The two key valuation multiples for both banks and insurance firms are P / E (Price Per Share / Earnings Per Share) and P / BV (Price Per Share / Book Value Per Share). “Book Value” just means Shareholders’ Equity, occasionally with some adjustments.

Concept note-3: -What Is the Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio? Many investors use the price-to-book ratio (P/B ratio) to compare a firm’s market capitalization to its book value and locate undervalued companies. This ratio is calculated by dividing the company’s current stock price per share by its book value per share (BVPS).

Concept note-4: -Market Multiples The market multiple approach is the simplest way to value a bank. A common multiple used by bank analysts is the Price-Earnings ratio (P/E).
