to conclude, does islamic finance achieve the ultimate objective of shariah

(A) ** ambiguous depend on the way the development is conceptualised

(B) no because the the inequality still rising

(C) yes because the muslim can use the halal financing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These objectives serve the financial needs of human beings. They include the circulation of wealth in the society, enjoining fair and transparent financial practices and promoting socio-economic justice. Wealth circulation comprises all processes related to its creation, consumption and distribution.

Concept note-2: -Shariah, and very much in the context of Islamic finance, emphasises justice and partnership. The main principles of Islamic finance are that: Wealth must be generated from legitimate trade and asset-based investment. (The use of money for the purposes of making money is expressly forbidden.)

Concept note-3: -There are five Maqasid (objectives) of Shariah: Protection of life, protection of property, protection of health, protection of religion, and protection of dignity.

Concept note-4: -The Islamic banking system is, in principle, compatible with a close correspondence between financial and real rates of return and an efficient allocation of resources. Moreover, it could be adopted without harming the effectiveness of official supervision of financial intermediation.
