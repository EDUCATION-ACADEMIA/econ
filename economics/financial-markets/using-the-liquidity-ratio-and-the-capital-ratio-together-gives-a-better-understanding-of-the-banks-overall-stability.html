Using the ‘liquidity ratio’ and the ‘capital ratio’ together gives a better understanding of the bank’s overall stability.

(A) ** Yes, I understand this from the notes

(B) No, I don’t understand this from the notes

(C) No, I don’t understand this, as I have not read the notes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Importance of Liquidity Ratio It helps understand the availability of cash in a company which determines the short term financial position of the company. A higher number is indicative of a sound financial position, while lower numbers show signs of financial distress.

Concept note-2: -Liquidity is a measure of the cash and other assets banks have available to quickly pay bills and meet short-term business and financial obligations. Capital is a measure of the resources banks have to absorb losses.

Concept note-3: -The working capital ratio is calculated simply by dividing total current assets by total current liabilities. For that reason, it can also be called the current ratio. It is a measure of liquidity, meaning the business’s ability to meet its payment obligations as they fall due.

Concept note-4: -Also known as the acid test ratio or cash ratio, the quick ratio is a good indicator of your company’s short-term liquidity. It tells you how many times liquid assets could be used to pay down your debt.
