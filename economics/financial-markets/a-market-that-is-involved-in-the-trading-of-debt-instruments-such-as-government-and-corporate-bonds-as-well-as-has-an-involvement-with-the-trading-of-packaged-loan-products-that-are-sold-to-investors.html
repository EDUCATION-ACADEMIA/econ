FINANCIAL MARKETS

[SOURCES]
A market that is involved in the trading of debt instruments such as government and corporate bonds, as well as has an involvement with the trading of packaged loan products that are sold to investors.

(A) ** Debt Market

(B) Equity Market

(C) Auction Market

(D) Over-the-counter Market

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The bond market-often called the debt market, fixed-income market, or credit market-is the collective name given to all trades and issues of debt securities. Governments typically issue bonds in order to raise capital to pay down debts or fund infrastructural improvements.

Concept note-2: -5.1 The government securities market is at the core of financial markets in most countries. It deals with tradeable debt instruments issued by the Government for meeting its financing requirements.

Concept note-3: -A capital market is a financial market in which long-term debt (over a year) or equity-backed securities are bought and sold, in contrast to a money market where short-term debt is bought and sold.

Concept note-4: -The debt market is the market where debt instruments are traded. Debt instruments are assets that require a fixed payment to the holder, usually with interest. Examples of debt instruments include bonds (government or corporate) and mortgages.
