When did RTE ACT come into force?

(A) 1st April 2011

(B) ** 1st April 2010

(C) 1st April 2012

(D) 1st April 2009

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Article 21-A and the RTE Act came into effect on 1 April 2010. The title of the RTE Act incorporates the words ‘free and compulsory’.

Concept note-2: -Inclusive Education Programmes This act provides a legal framework that entitles all children between the ages of 6-14 years free and compulsory admission, attendance and completion of elementary education. Section 3 (2) of the RTE Act lays impetus on the elementary education of children with disabilities.

Concept note-3: -1.1 Introduction The Act provides that all children in the age group of six to fourteen years have the right to free and compulsory education in a neighbourhood school till the completion of his or her elementary education. Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA)1 is the main vehicle for implementing the provisions of the Act.

Concept note-4: -It is the 86th amendment in the Indian Constitution. A rough draft of the bill was prepared in year 2005.

Concept note-5: -Main Features of Right to Education (RTE) Act, 2009 Free and compulsory education to all children of India in the 6 to 14 age group. No child shall be held back, expelled or required to pass a board examination until the completion of elementary education.
