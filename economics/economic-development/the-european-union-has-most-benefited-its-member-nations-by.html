The European Union has most benefited its member nations by

(A) ** reducing barriers to trade and travel

(B) sharing expense of a common defense

(C) promoting the use of a common language

(D) providing funds and materials for education

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is option 1 i.e Reducing barriers to trade and travel. When the EU was founded in 1957, the Member States concentrated on building a ‘common market’ for trade. Since 1957, the European Union has benefited its citizens by working for peace and prosperity.

Concept note-2: -Stimulus to GDP growth, more jobs, higher wages and pensions; Growing internal market and domestic demand; Free movement of labour, goods, services and capital; Free access to 450 million consumers.

Concept note-3: -BERLIN (Reuters)-The European Union’s industrial heartlands, its urban regions and Germany are the biggest beneficiaries of the bloc’s single market, according to a study that highlights the economic and social inequalities plaguing the bloc.

Concept note-4: -Benefits of EU trade to consumers 1. Decrease in prices for the same range of goods (direct); 2. Increase in the variety of products (direct); 3. Increase in the growth rate (indirect).

Concept note-5: -a continent at peace. freedom for its citizens to live, study or work anywhere in the EU. the world’s biggest single market. aid and development assistance for millions of people worldwide.
