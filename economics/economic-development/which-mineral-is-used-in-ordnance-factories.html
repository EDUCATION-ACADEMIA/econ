Which mineral is used in ordnance factories?

(A) Copper

(B) Gypsum

(C) ** Sulphur

(D) Salt

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sulfurâ€™s main use is in making chemicals for agriculture, mostly for fertilizers. Other uses of sulfur include refining petroleum, metal mining, and the production of organic and inorganic chemicals.

Concept note-2: -Sulphur is a common deposition product from volcanic gases associated with realgar, cinnabar and other minerals. It is also found in some vein deposits and as an alteration product of sulphide minerals.

Concept note-3: -It is found in many minerals and ores, e.g., iron pyrites, galena, cinnabar, zinc blende, gypsum, barite, and epsom salts and in mineral springs and other waters. It is found uncombined in some volcanic regions and in large underground deposits in Sicily and in Texas and Louisiana.

Concept note-4: -Sulfur is a macro element, meaning it is one of the most abundant minerals in the human body. It is the third most important mineral in the body, after calcium and phosphorus. The adult body contains approximately 140 mg, predominantly in the form of proteins such as keratin.
