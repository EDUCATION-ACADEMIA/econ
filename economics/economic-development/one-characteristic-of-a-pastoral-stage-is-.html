One characteristic of a Pastoral Stage is ____

(A) They only hunt for their food.

(B) They always have extra food.

(C) ** They domesticate animals for food.

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Major characteristics of a pastoral society include herding of animals as the primary means of subsistence, nomadic or semi-nomadic lifestyles, and little reliance upon agriculture.

Concept note-2: -For nearly 10, 000 years, pastoralists have domesticated and managed grazing animals such as reindeer, camels, yaks, alpacas, goats, sheep, and cattle. These animals, KÃ¶hler-Rollefson explains, are integral to humanity if we are to survive in increasingly hot, dry, and unpredictable climates.

Concept note-3: -In pastoral societies, people raise and herd sheep, goats, camels, and other domesticated animals and use them as their major source of food and also, depending on the animal, as a means of transportation. Some societies are either primarily horticultural or pastoral, while other societies combine both forms.

Concept note-4: -Fulfils food and dairy needs. Helps to reuse animal waste. Lessens import requirements. Better quality yield. Profitable Income. 15-Nov-2022
