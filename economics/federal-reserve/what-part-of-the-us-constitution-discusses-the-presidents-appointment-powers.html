What part of the US Constitution discusses the President’s appointment powers?

(A) Article I

(B) ** Article II

(C) Article III

(D) The Constitution doesn’t mention this.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Section 3 of Article Two lays out the responsibilities of the president, granting the president the power to convene both houses of Congress, receive foreign representatives, and commission all federal officers.

Concept note-2: -The President, Vice President and all civil Officers of the United States, shall be removed from Office on Impeachment for, and Conviction of, Treason, Bribery, or other high Crimes and Misdemeanors.

Concept note-3: -Overview: Article II Section 2 of the U.S. Constitution, the Commander in Chief clause, states that “[t]he President shall be Commander in Chief of the Army and Navy of the United States, and of the Militia of the several States, when called into the actual Service of the United States."

Concept note-4: -The Constitution, Article 2, Section 5 provides: ‘Section 5. The maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty; and property, and promotion of tbe general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people ofthe blessings of democracy.
