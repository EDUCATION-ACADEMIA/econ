Which of the following is one of the main parts of the Federal Reserve System?

(A) Board of Operators

(B) New York Stock Exchange

(C) ** Federal Open Market Committee

(D) stock market

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) consists of twelve members–the seven members of the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System; the president of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York; and four of the remaining eleven Reserve Bank presidents, who serve one-year terms on a rotating basis.

Concept note-2: -There are three key entities in the Federal Reserve System: the Board of Governors, the Federal Reserve Banks (Reserve Banks), and the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC).

Concept note-3: -To fulfill its mandate, the Fed’s most important lever is the buying or selling of U.S. Treasury bonds in the open market to influence banking reserves and interest rates. For instance, the Fed’s purchase of bonds puts more money into the financial system and thus reduces the cost of borrowing.

Concept note-4: -The Fed’s main duties include conducting national monetary policy, supervising and regulating banks, maintaining financial stability, and providing banking services.

Concept note-5: -Overview of the Federal Reserve System. The Federal Reserve performs five key functions in the public interest to promote the health of the U.S. economy and the stability of the U.S. financial system. Conducting Monetary Policy. Supervising and Regulating Financial Institutions and Activities. 20-Jan-2023
