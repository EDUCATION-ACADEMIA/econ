To finance the American Revolution, the first paper notes were produced were known as?

(A) Bank Notes

(B) U.S. Notes

(C) Greenbacks

(D) ** Continentals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Beginning in 1775, the Continental Congress issued currency to finance the Revolutionary War. These notes, called Continentals, had no backing in gold or silver.

Concept note-2: -Continentals refers to paper currency issued by the Continental Congress in 1775 to help fund the American Revolutionary War. Continentals quickly lost value, partly because they were not backed by a physical asset like gold or silver, but also due to the fact that too many bills were printed.

Concept note-3: -Continental currency, serial no. 41479, issued by Congress on 10 May 1775 for four dollars. Signed by S. Morris and Thomas Morris.

Concept note-4: -The first paper money issued by the government were “demand notes” commonly referred to as “greenbacks.” In 1862, Congress retired the demand notes and began issuing United States notes, also called legal tender notes.

Concept note-5: -Paul Revere of Boston engraved the plates for the first of these bills, which were known as Continental Currency.
