Uncle Jed’s Barbershop taught about the importance of

(A) saving

(B) working hard

(C) sharing

(D) ** all of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Students listen to the book Uncle Jed’s Barbershop, about an African-American barber who, despite significant setbacks, saves enough money to buy his own barbershop. From the story, students learn about saving, savings goals, opportunity cost, and segregation.

Concept note-2: -How did Uncle Jed help Sarah Jean? (He took the money out of his savings to pay for her operation.)

Concept note-3: -Despite obstacles in his life such as a family illness and the Great Depression, Uncle Jed achieved his dream of opening a barbershop at age 79.

Concept note-4: -PreSchool-Grade 3-Uncle Jed, a black, itinerant barber in the pre-Depression South, dreams of opening his own shop.
