Are the tax rates higher in the United States or in Western Europe?

(A) ** the United States

(B) Western Europe

(C) Taxes are very high in both the United States and in Western Europe.

(D) Taxes are very low in both the United States and in Western Europe.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The United States’ top individual tax rate is 37%. That’s lower than the top rate in 17 out of 27 European countries. 2 The countries with the highest tax rates were Denmark (56%) and Austria (55%).

Concept note-2: -Below are the maximum personal income tax rates as of 2022 for every EU state (in descending order): Finland – 56.95% Denmark – 55.90% Austria – 55.00%

Concept note-3: -US, Germany’s tax rate is relatively high in comparison. Though you pay more to German tax authorities up front, the benefit you gain is savings on your US tax return when filing with the IRS as an American expat living in Germany. Taxable income in Germany is employment income, post allowable and standard deductions.
