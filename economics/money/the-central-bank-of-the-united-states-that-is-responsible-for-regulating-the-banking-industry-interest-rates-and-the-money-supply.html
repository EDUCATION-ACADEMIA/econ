The central bank of the United States that is responsible for regulating the banking industry, interest rates, and the money supply

(A) ** Federal Reserve

(B) Checking Account

(C) Federal Reserve Note

(D) Savings Account

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1. Overview of the Federal Reserve System. The Federal Reserve performs five key functions in the public interest to promote the health of the U.S. economy and the stability of the U.S. financial system.

Concept note-2: -The Fed’s main duties include conducting national monetary policy, supervising and regulating banks, maintaining financial stability, and providing banking services.

Concept note-3: -It is responsible for managing monetary policy and regulating the financial system. It does this by setting interest rates, influencing the supply of money in the economy, and, in recent years, making trillions of dollars in asset purchases to boost financial markets.

Concept note-4: -About the Federal Reserve System The Federal Reserve System is the central bank of the United States.
