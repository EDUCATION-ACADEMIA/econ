COMPETITION AND MARKET STRUCTURES

[SOURCES]
A monopolist will be productively and allocatively efficient in long run equilibrium

(A) TRUE

(B) ** FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A monopolistically competitive industry does not display productive and allocative efficiency in either the short run, when firms are making economic profits and losses, nor in the long run, when firms are earning zero profits.

Concept note-2: -Monopoly is productively efficient (no other firm in the market so industry has the same MC). It does not have allocative efficiency because P > MC. The only exception to this rule is a monopoly with perfect price discrimination that will charge every consumer the maximum that they are willing to pay.

Concept note-3: -In the long-run, a monopolistically competitive market is inefficient. It achieves neither allocative nor productive efficiency.

Concept note-4: -Long-run equilibrium in perfectly competitive markets meets two important conditions: allocative efficiency and productive efficiency. These two conditions have important implications. First, resources are allocated to their best alternative use. Second, they provide the maximum satisfaction attainable by society.
