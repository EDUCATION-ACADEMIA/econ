If the marginal propensity to consume (MPC) is 0.75, the value of the multiplier is

(A) 0.75

(B) ** 4

(C) 5

(D) 7.5

(E) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If the MPC is 0.75, the Keynesian government spending multiplier will be 4/3; that is, an increase of $ 300 billion in government spending will lead to an increase in GDP of $ 400 billion. The multiplier is 1 / (1-MPC) = 1 / MPS = 1 /0.25 = 4.

Concept note-2: -If the marginal propensity to consume is 0.75, the value of the spending multiplier will be 5.

Concept note-3: -Therefore, if K= 4, MPC= 3/4 or 0.75.

Concept note-4: -If out of it, he spends 70 paise on consumption (i.e., MPC = 0.7) and saves 30 paise (i.e., MPS = 0 3) then MPC + MPS = 0.7 + 0.3 = 1.

Concept note-5: -Use the MPC formula Using the above example, you can divide $3, 500 by $5, 000, which equals 0.7. This means that the consumer in the example spent 70% of every new dollar of disposable income they earned.
