If you have a low tolerance for risk, you would most likely invest your money in

(A) stocks.

(B) junk bonds.

(C) ** government bonds.

(D) corporate bonds.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An aggressive investor, or one with a high risk tolerance, is willing to risk losing money to get potentially better results. A conservative investor, or one with a low risk tolerance, favors investments that maintain his or her original investment.

Concept note-2: -Knowing the risk tolerance level helps investors plan their entire portfolio and will drive how they invest. For example, if an individual’s risk tolerance is low, investments will be made conservatively and will include more low-risk investments and less high-risk investments.

Concept note-3: -The investment type that typically carries the least risk is a savings account. CDs, bonds, and money market accounts could be grouped in as the least risky investment types around. These financial instruments have minimal market exposure, which means they’re less affected by fluctuations than stocks or funds.
