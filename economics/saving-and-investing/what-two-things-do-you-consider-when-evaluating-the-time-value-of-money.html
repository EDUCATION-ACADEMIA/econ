What two things do you consider when evaluating the time value of money

(A) time and savings

(B) time and interest rate

(C) ** inflation and interest rate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The time value of money takes several things into account when calculating the future value of money, including the present value of money (PV), the number of compounding periods per year (n), the total number of years (t), and the interest rate (i).

Concept note-2: -The exact time value of money is determined by two factors: Opportunity Cost, and Interest Rates.

Concept note-3: -In general, you calculate the time value of money by assessing a discount factor of future value factor to a set of cash flows. The factor is determined by the number of periods the cash flow will impacted as well as the expected rate of interest for the period.

Concept note-4: -The equation goes like this: PV = FV (1+i)^-n, where PV equals present value, FV equals future value, i equals annual inflation, and n equals number of years. Assuming an inflation rate of 3% (or 0.03), the equation looks like this: PV = $100, 000 * 1.03^-3. The present value of $100, 000 in three years is $91, 514.
