EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bonds are debt instruments in which the investor loans money to an entity. The entity borrows money at a fixed interest rate for a specific time duration. Such an entity can be government, banks or corporates. Hence, when the government issues bonds, they are known as government bonds.

Concept note-2: -Treasury bonds are long-term bonds with a maturity of 30 years. T-Bonds provide semiannual interest payments and usually have $1, 000 face values. A 30-year Treasury bond was issued on February 15, 2023, with a rate of 3.625%.

Concept note-3: -Step-up bonds provide investors with periodic interest payments while allowing them the chance to earn a higher rate in the future. Some bonds are single step-up bonds that have only one increase in the coupon rate, while others may have multi-step increases.

Concept note-4: -The coupon rate is the rate of interest the bond issuer will pay on the face value of the bond, expressed as a percentage.1 For example, a 5% coupon rate means that bondholders will receive 5% x $1, 000 face value = $50 every year. Coupon dates are the dates on which the bond issuer will make interest payments.
