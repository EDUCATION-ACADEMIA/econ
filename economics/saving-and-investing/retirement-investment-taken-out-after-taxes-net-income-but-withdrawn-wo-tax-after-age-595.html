[SOURCES]
Retirement investment taken out after taxes (net income) but withdrawn w/o tax after age 59.5

(A) Traditional IRA

(B) Municipal Bond

(C) 403(b)

(D) ** Roth IRA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In order to guarantee that the benefits of IRAs are used solely for retirement, the IRS imposes age limits on these accounts. Unless users are willing to incur a 10% penalty, IRA assets are not accessible until age 59 and a half.

Concept note-2: -Once you reach age 59.5, you may withdraw money from your 401(k) penalty-free. If you tap into it beforehand, you may face a 10% penalty tax on the withdrawal in addition to income tax that you’d owe on any type of withdrawal from a traditional 401(k).

Concept note-3: -Age 59½ and over: No withdrawal restrictions Once you reach age 59½, you can withdraw funds from your Traditional IRA without restrictions or penalties.

Concept note-4: -Recall that withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts are subject to ordinary income taxes, which can be taxed at federal rates of up to 37%. And if you tap these accounts prior to age 59½, the withdrawal may be subject to a 10% federal tax penalty (barring certain exceptions).
