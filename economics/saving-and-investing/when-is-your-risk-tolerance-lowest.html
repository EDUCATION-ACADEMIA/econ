When is your risk tolerance lowest?

(A) ** when you are closest to needing the money you invested

(B) when you are concerned about the diversity of your investment portfolio

(C) when you are an inexperienced investor

(D) when you first make the investment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The sooner you need the money, the lower your risk tolerance should be.

Concept note-2: -An aggressive investor, or one with a high risk tolerance, is willing to risk losing money to get potentially better results. A conservative investor, or one with a low risk tolerance, favors investments that maintain his or her original investment.

Concept note-3: -Savings, CDs, Money Market Accounts, and Bonds Some that are considered the safest also generate the least interest (or returns). The investment type that typically carries the least risk is a savings account. CDs, bonds, and money market accounts could be grouped in as the least risky investment types around.
