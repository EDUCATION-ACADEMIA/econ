Concept note-2: -Typically, yes-your money is yours. But a savings account is designed to discourage frequent transactional use and may carry monthly withdrawal limits. Exceeding these limits can incur fees, have your account re-classified or have it closed altogether.

Concept note-3: -Getting Cash is Simple Regardless of the reason, it’s your money and you can easily access it through several different methods, such as using an ATM, cashing a check, making an in-person withdrawal at the teller window, or using a debit card.

Concept note-4: -The primary difference between a money market account and a savings account is how you can access your money. With a money market account, you’ll typically get a checkbook and/or debit card. You can write checks against the account’s balance or use the debit card to make purchases and withdraw money from ATMs.

Concept note-5: -Making an ATM withdrawal couldn’t be easier. Just insert your physical card or scan your virtual one over the reader, select the language option, and enter your 4-digit PIN number. Once the PIN is approved, select ‘withdrawal’ for transaction type, and enter the amount you want to withdraw.
