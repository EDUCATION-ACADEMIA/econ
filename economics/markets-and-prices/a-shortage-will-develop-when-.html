A shortage will develop when ____

(A) The quantity supplied of a good is greater than the quantity demanded

(B) The Equilibrium quantity supplied is lower than actual quantity supplied

(C) The government provides subsidies to producers

(D) ** The market price is below the equilibrium price

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is A price ceiling below the equilibrium price often leads to a Shortage of commodity and black marketing. When a price ceiling is set below the equilibrium price, quantity demanded will exceed quantity supplied, and excess demand or shortages will result.

Concept note-2: -Just as a price above the equilibrium price will cause a surplus, a price below equilibrium will cause a shortage. A shortage is the amount by which the quantity demanded exceeds the quantity supplied at the current price.

Concept note-3: -In the face of a shortage, sellers are likely to begin to raise their prices. As the price rises, there will be an increase in the quantity supplied (but not a change in supply) and a reduction in the quantity demanded (but not a change in demand) until the equilibrium price is achieved.

Concept note-4: -Conversely, if the price of a good is below equilibrium, then it must be that the quantity of the good demanded exceeds the quantity of the good supplied-meaning that there is a shortage of the good (at the existing price).
